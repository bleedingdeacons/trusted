Telephone Rota

A step-by-step guide to running the telephone responder rota — building weekly shift templates, filling the calendar, assigning responders, and handling callers who ask to be contacted.

What is Trusted?

Trusted is a planner for the telephone helpline: you build reusable weekly shift patterns, apply them to any week, and put a telephone responder on each shift from a calendar view.

Trusted does not have its own list of people. Everyone you can place on a shift comes from the shared member records — specifically, the members who have been marked as telephone responders. So the first job, before any rota work, is making sure the right people are flagged correctly. That is covered in Setting Up Members.

⚠️
Personal data. A responder's mobile number and email appear on their shift so whoever is coordinating can reach them. These are private contact details — the Scrutiny plugin logs access to them. Use them only for rota purposes and never share them outside the system.

How the Pieces Fit Together

Three different things are easy to confuse. They are set independently on a member's record, and a person can be any combination of them.

RoleWhat it meansWhere it is used
Telephone Responder Available to take a turn answering the helpline. These are the only people who can be put on the Trusted rota. The Trusted rota calendar and templates.
12th Stepper Available to be contacted directly for a 12th-step call. Has an area they cover and the forms of contact they accept. The public finder, and the Call Requests list (see Requests to Contact).
GSR / Officer Service positions in the intergroup. Unrelated to the rota. The intergroup admin — not Trusted.
💡
A responder answering the phone often also needs to reach a 12th stepper on the caller's behalf — so the two roles work together, but they are still ticked separately. Tick both on a member who does both jobs.

Making a Member a 12th Stepper

Intergroup › Members › [Member name]

The 12th-stepper flag is separate from the responder flag. A 12th stepper is someone who agrees to be contacted for a 12th-step call. They are the people who show up on the public finder and in the Call Requests list. Set this up if you want a member to receive callback requests.

  1. Go to Intergroup › Members and open (or add) the member record.
  2. Scroll to the Home group.
  3. Set the member's Home Group (required). The 12th Stepper switch stays hidden until a home group is set.
  4. Switch 12th Stepper to On.
  5. Set the Area (required) — the part of the region they can cover for a 12th-step call. The finder uses this to match a caller to the nearest available member.
  6. Tick the Accepts options (required — at least one) — the callers this member is willing to take 12th-step calls from. Tick Male to accept male callers and Female to accept female callers. A third option, Non-binary, is only set with the 12th stepper's permission. Leave any they do not want unticked.
  7. In the About group, make sure the Mobile Number / Personal Email the member wants to be reached on are present.
  8. Click Update.
FieldWhat to enter
Home Group*Required. The member's home group. A 12th stepper must have a home group — the 12th Stepper switch is hidden until one is set.
12th Stepper*Switch On to make the member available for 12th-step calls.
Area*Required. The geographic area the member covers. This can be an area name (e.g. Kingswood) or a postcode (e.g. BS15). Cover more than one place by separating them with a | (pipe), e.g. Kingswood|Hanham or BS15|BS5. Each is matched against the caller's location and the nearest one is used to find the closest available member.
Accepts*Required (at least one). The callers the member will take 12th-step calls from. Tick Male for male callers and Female for female callers. The third option, Non-binary, is only set with the 12th stepper's permission. The finder matches these against the caller's own selection.
⚠️
A 12th stepper must have a home group. Make sure the Home Group is set — the 12th Stepper switch is hidden if not.
🔒
Consent matters. Only flag a member as a 12th stepper with their agreement — it makes them contactable by people they have not met. The same goes for the contact details on their record.
💡
Doing both jobs? Many people answer the phone and take 12th-step calls. Just turn on both toggles — Telephone Responder in the Service group and Is Twelfth Stepper? in the Home group — and save once.

About Shift Templates

A template is a reusable weekly pattern of shifts — for example "three desks a day, Monday to Sunday". Build it once, then apply it to any week to create that week's shifts in a couple of clicks, instead of typing every shift out by hand.

You don't have to use templates

Templates are a time-saver, not a requirement. You can always add shifts straight onto a week in the calendar (see Adding & Removing Shifts). Templates simply save you repeating the same pattern every week.

Creating a Template

Trusted › Shift Templates › Add Template
  1. Go to Trusted › Shift Templates and click Add Template.
  2. Give the template a clear title — for example Standard week or Bank holiday cover. You'll pick it by this name later.
  3. You'll see a box for each day, Monday to Sunday. In each day, type one shift per line (the exact format is explained below). Leave a day empty for no shifts that day.
  4. Click Publish.

The editor is an Advanced Custom Fields form. The post Title names the template; the Weekly Shift Pattern group holds one box per day, and you type one shift per line. Leave a day empty for no shifts:

The Shift Line Format

Each line in a day's box describes one shift. The pattern is:

HH:MM-HH:MM | Shift name | Member

Times come first, then an optional name, then an optional member — separated by the | (pipe) character. For example:

09:00-13:00 | Reception | John D
13:00-17:00 | Reception
17:00-21:00 | Evening on-call
PartRules
Times* 24-hour clock, from 00:00 to 24:00. Use 00:00 for the start of the day and 24:00 for the very end — so a whole day is 00:00-24:00.
Shift name* A short label such as Reception or Evening on-call. Required — a line with no name will not save.
Member Optional. The member's anonymous name. If you name someone they must be an existing telephone responder or the template won't save — and they are assigned automatically when the template is applied.
⚠️
Naming a member who isn't a responder. If a line names someone who is not flagged as a telephone responder, the whole template refuses to save. Either flag them first (see Make a Telephone Responder) or leave the member off the line and assign them later in the calendar.

Save a Week as a Template

If you have already built a good week by hand in the calendar, you can turn it into a reusable template without retyping it.

  1. On the Rota Calendar, navigate to the week you want to capture.
  2. Click Save week as template.
  3. Type a template name.
  4. Leave Include assigned members ticked to carry the people across too, or untick it to save just the shift pattern (times and names) with no one assigned.
  5. Click Save. The new template appears in the dropdown immediately.
💡
Including members only works for people who are still telephone responders at the moment you save. It's a handy way to clone a "typical week" and reuse it going forward.

Moving Between Weeks

The toolbar at the top of the calendar shows the dates of the week you are viewing, with three buttons beneath:

  • ← Previous — jump back one week.
  • This week — return to the current week.
  • Next → — jump forward one week.

There is also a ⟳ Refresh button next to the "Telephone Rota" title. Use it to reload the latest data — for example after you've just flagged a new responder and want them to appear in the dropdown.

💡
The calendar remembers the last week you looked at, so when you come back it reopens on that week rather than snapping to today.

Applying a Template to a Week

This fills a week from one of your saved templates in one step.

  1. Navigate to the week you want to fill.
  2. In the toolbar, pick a template from the Select Template dropdown.
  3. Click Apply template. The week's shifts are created, and any members named in the template are assigned automatically.
✓
Applying is safe by default. Shifts that already exist on a date and time are left exactly as they are — names and assignments kept. Only new shifts are added, so re-applying a template never overwrites or duplicates what's already there.
🗑️
Replace existing slots this week. Tick this box before clicking Apply only when you want to wipe the whole week first and rebuild it from the template. This removes the week's existing shifts and their assignments. Leave it unticked for the normal, safe behaviour.

Adding & Removing Shifts

Add a shift

  1. At the bottom of a day's column, click Add Shift. (Or double-click a Gap marker to start with that gap's times pre-filled.)
  2. Enter a Shift name (required).
  3. Set the Start and End times as HH:MM (anything from 00:00 to 24:00).
  4. Optionally pick a Member to assign straight away — or leave it blank and assign later.
  5. Click Save. The shift drops into the column in time order.

Remove a shift

Click the × on a shift's time row, then confirm.

⚠️
You can only delete an empty shift. While someone is assigned, the × is hidden — remove the person first (see below), then the shift can be deleted.

Assigning a Responder to a Shift

Each shift holds one responder.

  1. On an open shift, click Assign.
  2. Choose a person from the — Select a member — dropdown. The list contains only telephone responders, sorted by name.
  3. Click Assign. Their name, phone and email now show on the shift.

Swapping who's on a shift

  1. Click the × next to the current person's name and confirm — the shift becomes open again.
  2. Click Assign and pick the replacement.
💡
The same responder can be on as many different shifts and days as you like — each shift is managed on its own. Assigning one shift never affects another.
⚠️
Someone missing from the dropdown? They are not flagged as a telephone responder. Set them up (see Make a Telephone Responder), then click ⟳ Refresh.

Assigning One Member to Many Shifts

When the same person is covering several shifts — say a whole weekend — assign them in one go instead of shift by shift.

  1. In the toolbar, click Assign member to shifts. The calendar switches to selection mode and a bar appears at the top.
  2. In the bar, pick the person from the — Select a member — dropdown.
  3. Tick each open shift you want to give them. (Click anywhere on an open shift card to tick it.) The bar shows a running count of how many are selected.
  4. Click Assign.
  5. Click Cancel to leave selection mode at any time.
💡
Already-filled shifts appear dimmed and can't be ticked — bulk assign only fills empty shifts. If any selected shift was filled by someone else in the meantime, it's quietly skipped and you're told how many were left unchanged.

Clearing a Week

Two week-level clean-up buttons appear in the toolbar, depending on the state of the week. Both ask you to confirm and cannot be undone.

ButtonWhen it showsWhat it does
Clear week The week has shifts but no one is assigned yet. Deletes every shift for the week — a clean slate before you rebuild it.
Delete week's assignments At least one person is assigned somewhere in the week. Removes everybody from their shifts but keeps the shifts themselves, ready to be re-staffed.
⚠️
These act on the whole visible week. To change a single shift, use the per-shift × instead.

What a "Request to Contact" Is

When someone uses the public finder out of hours and asks to be called back, the system records a call request: a note that a particular 12th stepper should phone the caller. These requests are the bridge between a member of the public reaching out and a 12th stepper making contact.

💡
Call requests are about 12th steppers, not the telephone rota. A person only appears here if they've been flagged as a 12th stepper — see Make a 12th Stepper.

Working a request

  1. Go to Reach › Call requests.
  2. Read the 12th Stepper, Caller, Phone and any Notes. The phone number is a clickable link.
  3. Make sure the request is actioned — i.e. the 12th stepper is told to call the person back (your intergroup's usual process).
  4. Once it's been dealt with, click Delete on that row to clear it from the list.
⚠️
This is sensitive data. The list holds a caller's name and phone number. Only people with permission to view personal data can open it, access is logged, and requests are automatically removed after a retention period. Delete each one as soon as it has been actioned — don't leave them sitting.

Troubleshooting

A responder isn't in the assign dropdown

The dropdown only lists members flagged as telephone responders. Open the member at Intergroup › Members, scroll to the Service group, switch Telephone Responder on, and save.

Back on the calendar, click ⟳ Refresh — the dropdown reloads and they'll appear.

A template won't save

Two common causes: a shift line with no name, or a line that names a member who isn't a telephone responder. Add a name to every line, and either flag the named person as a responder first or remove them from the line.

Check times are written HH:MM-HH:MM between 00:00 and 24:00.

I can't delete a shift

A shift with someone assigned hides its × on purpose. Remove the person first (× next to their name), then the shift's × reappears.

Applying a template didn't change anything

Applying is non-destructive — it only adds shifts that don't already exist. If the week already had those shifts, nothing visibly changes. To rebuild the week from scratch, tick Replace existing slots this week before clicking Apply.

The Telephone menu is missing entirely

Either your account lacks the required permission, or the Unity plugin (which supplies the member list) is inactive — Trusted shows an admin notice and does nothing without it. Ask your site administrator to check both.

A member's name on a shift is out of date

Shift cards show the member's current anonymous name. Update it on the member record at Intergroup › Members, save, then ⟳ Refresh the calendar.
